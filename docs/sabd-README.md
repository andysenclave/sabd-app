# Sabd

*A word you almost know. Sixty seconds to prove you do.*

---

## What is Sabd?

**Sabd** is a competitive word-guessing game. You pick a topic, a row of empty boxes
appears — one box per letter — and a 60-second clock starts running. Your job: guess the
hidden word before time runs out.

You start with nothing. No letters, no clue — just the number of boxes telling you how
long the word is. If you're stuck, you can spend hints, but every hint costs you precious
seconds. Guess it fast with no help and you look like a genius. Burn all your hints and
scrape it with two seconds left and, well, you still got there.

The name comes from **śabda (शब्द)** — the Sanskrit word for "word." In Indian philosophy,
śabda is also considered a genuine source of knowledge in its own right. A game built
entirely around words felt like it deserved the name.

---

## How a round works

1. **Pick a topic.** Gaming, Space & Sci-Fi, Music, Internet & Tech Culture, Food & Drink,
   or World & Places.
2. **The boxes appear.** Say it's a 6-letter word — you see six empty boxes, like the code
   entry screen when you get an OTP. Beside them sits a short, deliberately *vague*
   description of the word — a free nudge that sets the scene without giving it away. The
   clock starts at 60 seconds.
3. **Start guessing.** Type letters into the boxes and submit. Wrong? The boxes shake, the
   clock keeps ticking, try again.
4. **Stuck? Use a hint** — but it costs time. There are two:
   - **Position** — drops one letter into its exact box and locks it.
   - **Letters** — shows you two possible letters, one real and one fake. It narrows things
     down, but you still have to work out which is which and where it goes.
5. **Solve it** and the boxes light up, the clock freezes, and you score. **Run out of
   time** and the answer is revealed — no points this round.

Simple to learn in ten seconds. The depth comes from *how well* you play, not from complex
rules.

## The twist: it's ranked, like chess

This is what makes Sabd different from every "guess the word" app.

**Every player has a rating** — a single number that goes up when you play well and down
when you don't. It works like a chess rating. And here's the clever part: **every word also
has a rating.** A common short word is "low-rated"; an obscure, tricky one is "high-rated."

So when you play solo, you're not just guessing a word — you're playing *against that
word's rating*. Beat a word rated higher than you, and your rating climbs. Fumble an easy
one, and it drops. The better you get, the harder the words Sabd serves you. You're always
matched to your level, always a little challenged, never bored.

Feeling confident? You can **deliberately ask for harder words** than your rating suggests,
or **challenge players rated above you** — for bigger rewards if you pull it off. Risk and
reward, built in.

Your rating isn't just a score. It's your identity in Sabd. It's the thing you want to see
go up.

## Ways to play

- **Solo** — you versus the word. Works anywhere, anytime, even with nobody else online.
  This is the heart of the game and it's always available.
- **Head-to-head (1v1)** — get matched against another real player. You both get the *exact
  same word* at the same time. Whoever cracks it first (with the fewest hints) wins more.
  Pure race, pure bragging rights.
- **Daily word** *(planned)* — one word, the same for everyone in the world, one shot per
  day. Compare your time against everyone else's. The reason you open the app every morning.

## Who it's for

People who like word games, trivia, and a bit of a competitive edge. The topics lean
modern and young on purpose — this isn't a school quiz. If you've ever lost an evening to
a word puzzle or shouted the answer at a trivia screen, Sabd is aimed at you.

It's **mobile-first** and built to be picked up for a single 60-second round while waiting
for a bus, or played for an hour chasing a rating climb.

---

## Why it's different

Plenty of word games exist. Here's what makes Sabd its own thing:

- **It's a race, not a crossword.** The clock is your opponent. Every round has tension.
- **A free nudge, then costly help.** Every word comes with a vague description for free —
  just enough to get your bearings. Beyond that, hints cost you time, so getting more help
  is always a real decision, never a free crutch.
- **It's genuinely ranked.** Most word apps give you a meaningless score. Sabd gives you a
  rating that means something, that matches you to the right difficulty, that you can
  actually climb — and defend.
- **The words grow with you.** The game gets harder as you get better, automatically.
  You're never stuck on baby words or crushed by impossible ones.
- **Modern topics, timeless words.** The categories feel current and youthful, but the
  answers are real words that won't feel dated next year.

---

## The feel we're going for

Fast. Tense. Satisfying. A round should feel like a tiny adrenaline hit — the boxes filling
in, the clock draining, that little rush when the right word snaps into place and everything
lights up. And when your rating ticks up afterward, that should feel *earned*.

Learn it in one round. Get hooked by the second. Chase the rating for weeks.

---

## Where things stand

Sabd is in early design. The core single-player round, the rating system, the word-content
pipeline, and the visual identity are being designed in parallel. Head-to-head and the daily
word come once the core loop feels great on its own.

*One word. Sixty seconds. How high can you climb?*
